Market

Problem, opportunity & reach

Vietnam's households need real-time behavioral visibility. Eco-Eye addresses a structural gap between awareness and action — with a go-to-market plan built for scale.

Market Pain Points

The Problem We're Solving

Vietnam's demand for electricity and clean water is growing rapidly. Yet household resource consumption remains largely unmonitored, unmanaged, and disconnected from the country's broader climate commitments. Three systemic barriers stand in the way.

01

Invisible Waste

Households consume energy and water without visibility into where the waste actually occurs. Without real-time feedback, inefficient behaviors persist unnoticed — lights left on, appliances left running, water flowing unnecessarily.

02

Awareness Without Action

Many households are aware of their environmental impact, yet still fail to change daily routines. Knowing is not enough — sustained behavior change requires consistent nudging, structured feedback loops, and positive reinforcement over time.

03

No Carbon Feedback Loop

People cannot visualize how their individual actions translate into CO₂ reductions. Without a direct, personal connection between daily behavior and climate impact, motivation to act sustainably remains abstract and short-lived.

The Market Gap

Vietnam faces increasing pressure to meet its climate commitments while managing surging household electricity and water demand. No existing consumer product in the Vietnamese market addresses the root cause: real-time behavioral visibility at the household level. Market Opportunity

Why the Time Is Now

Three converging forces are creating an urgent and rapidly expanding market for household sustainability technology in Vietnam.

Surging Resource Demand

Vietnam's rapid urbanization and economic growth are driving significant increases in household electricity and clean water consumption. Demand is rising faster than infrastructure and efficiency programs can offset, creating pressure at both the household and national level.

Supporting market data to be incorporated from research partners prior to investor presentation.

Vietnam's Climate Commitments

Vietnam has made formal climate pledges as part of its national development strategy, committing to measurable reductions in carbon emissions. These commitments create institutional momentum for sustainability solutions and growing consumer awareness of individual responsibility.

Rising Sustainability Consciousness

A growing segment of Vietnamese consumers — particularly urban, middle-class households — are actively seeking tools that help them live more sustainably. Cost savings and environmental responsibility are converging as purchase motivators, creating ideal market conditions for Eco-Eye.

Target Audience

Who Eco-Eye
Is Built For

Eco-Eye is designed for households that care about both their financial well-being and their environmental impact — and are ready for a tool that bridges the gap between good intentions and real, measurable action.

Urban Family Households

Households in Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi with children, seeking to reduce utility bills and model responsible living for the next generation.

Cost-Aware Homeowners

Middle-income households where electricity and water are a meaningful monthly expense, and where behavioral changes deliver tangible, measurable financial returns.

Sustainability-Minded Consumers

Environmentally conscious individuals who want to translate their values into measurable household action — and share that progress with their family and community.

Go-to-Market

Where Customers Find Eco-Eye

A three-channel distribution strategy ensures Eco-Eye reaches customers at multiple discovery touchpoints, with e-commerce as the primary and most scalable launch channel.

Priority Channel

E-Commerce

Direct-to-consumer sales through leading Vietnamese e-commerce platforms. This channel enables national reach from day one, low-overhead distribution, and rich behavioral data to inform product development.

  • Shopee, Lazada, and direct website
  • Targeted digital campaigns to sustainability segments
  • Unboxing and review content via influencer partnerships

Electronics Retail

Physical distribution through established electronics chains provides credibility and hands-on product discovery for consumers who prefer to evaluate smart home devices in person before purchasing.

  • Dedicated in-store demonstration zones
  • Trained retail staff for product education
  • Strategic placement in the smart home category

Smart Home Partners

Co-distribution and ecosystem integration with smart home system providers and property developers unlock premium, high-intent customer segments — particularly valuable for the Eco-Eye 3.0 smart home integration story.

  • Bundled offers with smart home system providers
  • Integration into property developer sustainability programs
  • B2B corporate sustainability package opportunities
Marketing Strategy

Messaging That Moves People

Three campaign pillars anchor the Eco-Eye brand — each designed to connect sustainability to personal identity, household responsibility, and community action.

I

"We don't just track carbon.
We change behavior."

Our core brand positioning — elevating Eco-Eye above the commodity smart home device category and establishing us clearly as a behavior change platform that happens to come in the form of a camera.

II

"Climate change starts
in our homes."

A direct appeal to personal responsibility. This pillar reframes climate action as something every household can and should participate in — not just governments, corporations, or distant institutions.

III

"One Day Without Waste,
with Eco-Eye."

A campaign-ready challenge format that drives trial, creates organic social sharing potential, and demonstrates measurable value within a single day. Designed for community activation and viral amplification.
